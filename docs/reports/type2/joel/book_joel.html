Joel: Sample, Best Representatives, Minor Recensions, Other Studies
  • Sample of the Text: Joel 1:1-20; 2:1-32; 3:1-21.
  • Sample Size: 100% of the book.
  • Sample of Manuscripts: 28. DAVAZ1_l15-e16th, EMIP881_17th, IES77_1934, EMIP1029_15th, UNES10.04_l17e18th, IES436_15-16th, UNES10.34_1931, UNES10.65_l18e19th, EMIP746_1719, EMIP1063_1889-1913, EMIP1095_19th, VatCer75_1930/1, EMIP949_e20th, Hunt625_14th, EMML2080_14th, EMIP2007_18th, EMML1481_e17th, EAP704-1-5_15th, EMML6686_17th, GG106_1694, GG152_16th, GG181_16th, BNAbb35_17th, BNAbb55_15-16th, EAP704-2-12_e16th, EAP704-2-6_16th, Fait1_17th, PrinceGarEth7_18th.
  • Number of Data Points Generated: 43,372.
  • Earliest Attested 1 Best Representative – Bod Hunt 625 (14th).
  • Earliest Attested 2 Best Representative – EAP 704.2.6 (16th).
  • Earliest Attested 3 Best Representative – PrinceGarEth7 (18th).
  • Earliest Attested 5 Best Representative – BNAbb35 (17th).
  • Standardized 1 Best Representative – EMIP2007 (18th).
  • Modern Textus Receptus Best Representative – VatCer75 (1930-31).
  • Minor Recensions: EMIP 949 (e20th), UNESCO 10.34 (1931), and EMML 7942 (20th, not a part of the sample) represent a minor recension, translated from the Latin Vulgate, with a 42% degree of difference from the rest of the tradition.
