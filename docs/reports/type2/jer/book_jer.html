Jeremiah: Sample, Best Representatives, Minor Recensions, Other Studies
  • Sample of the Text: Jeremiah 2: 1-37
  • Sample Size: 2.5% of the book.
  • Sample of Manuscripts: 34. Ber 3067 (16th), BerPeter42 (15th), BN Abb 035 (17th), BN Abb 055 (15th), Cam Add. 1570 (1588), BL Add. 24991 (17th), BL Or 496 (17th), EMIP 0682 (20th), EMIP 0827 (18th), EMIP 0937 (19th), EMIP 0945 (18th), EMIP 1029 (15th), EMIP 1051 (17th), EMIP 1083 (18th), EMIP 1091 (1921), EMIP 1105 (l18-e19th), EMML 0065 (e19th), EMML 2080 (14th), EMML 2082 (16-17th), EMML 8644 (15th), EMML 6686 (17), EMML 7584, EMML 8671, GG63(15th), GG106 (1682-1706), GG 183 (15th), IES 0077 (1934), IES 0722 (=EMML25), ML26 (14th), UNES2.16 (19th), UNES10.4, UNES10.12 (18th), UNES10.48 (e20th), Vat Cer 75 (1930)
  • Number of Data Points Generated: 32,572.
  • EA1 Best Representative – GG63 (15th). Here is a transcription of the manuscript.
  • EA2 Best Representative – BN Abb 55 (15-16th). Here is a transcription of the manuscript.
  • Stan1 Best Representative – GG 106 (1682-1706) and EMIP945 (18th) both represent the cluster well. Here is a transcription of the manuscript.
  • MTR Best Representative – IES77 (1934). Here is a transcription of the manuscript.
  • Minor Recension: CamAdd1570 (1588), UNESCO 10.4 (17-18th), BerPeter42 (15th), and ML26 (14-15th) all represent a minor recension with a 4% degree of difference from one another and a 45% degree of difference from the rest of the tradition.
