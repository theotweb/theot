Malachi: Sample, Best Representatives, Minor Recensions, Other Studies
  • Sample of the Text: Mal 1:4-6, 10-12, 14; 2:1-3, 7, 12-16; 3:1-2, 5-10, 14-15; 4:1, 4-5.
  • Sample Size: 59.5% of the book.
  • Sample of Manuscripts: 34. Hunt625 (14th), EMIP0746 (1719), EMIP0881 (17th), EMIP1029 (15/16), EMIP1063 (19/20), EMIP1070 (1914), EMIP1095 (19th), EMIP2007 (18th), IES077 (20th), IES436 (15/16), EMML36 (L18/e19), EMML1481 (e17), EMML1768 (L15), EMML6686 (17th), Cerulli75 (1930), AxumZion (15th), UN10.04 (17/18), UN10.65 (18/19), UN10.34 (19th), EMIP0949 (20th), PrinceGarEth7 (17th), Cam1570 (1588/9), EMML7584 (16th), GG152 (16th), EMML6930 (18th), GG106 (~1694), EMML349 (1923), EMML1842 (1662), EMML2080 (16th), EMML2440 (1663), EMML8934 (14th), Abb35 (17th), Abb55_15/16), JE5E (19th).
  • Number of Data Points Generated: 37,026.
  • Earliest Attested 1 Best Representative – Bod Hunt 625 (14th) and GG152 (16th) both represent the cluster well.
  • Earliest Attested 2 Best Representative – EMML 1768 (l15th).
  • Standardized 1 Best Representative – GG 106 (1694) and EMIP 746 (1719) both represent the cluster well.
  • Standardized 2 Best Representative – EMML 349 (1923).
  • Standardized 3 Best Representative – EMML 1481 (e17th) and BNAbb35 (17th) both represent the cluster well.
  • Modern Textus Receptus Best Representative – IES 77 (1934).
  • Minor Recensions: EMIP 949 (e20th), UNESCO 10.34 (1931), and EMML 7942 (20th, the latter is not part of this sample). represent a minor recension, translated from the Latin Vulgate, with a 55% degree of difference from the rest of the tradition.
